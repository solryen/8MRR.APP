What competitors teach us

Most dashboards in this category fall into one of three buckets:

Social media managers show calendars, scheduling queues, account-level analytics, competitor monitoring, and reports. Buffer/Sprout/Hootsuite-style products are broad social operating systems, not revenue-learning systems. Recent comparisons highlight scheduling, analytics, team workflows, competitor engagement, audience growth, content calendars, approvals, and reports as common features.  ￼

Creative intelligence tools like Foreplay/Motion focus on saving ads, competitor tracking, creative analytics, top hooks, and performance dashboards. They are built around paid creative intelligence and ad teams.  ￼

LarryLoop-style tools sell the loop: generate content, post it, track what drives revenue, and iterate winners. That is closest to our direction, but our V1 is narrower: carousels first, revenue-intent first.  ￼

So the dashboard should not look like a normal social scheduler. It should look like a revenue-driven creative lab.

Core dashboard principle

The Home page should answer only three questions:

What is working?
What should I make next?
What is wasting time?

Everything else lives behind tabs.

Final visual direction

Use a clean “lab dashboard” style.

Not playful. Not corporate.

Visual hierarchy:

dark/near-white background
large decision cards
colored score chips
minimal charts
clear decisions: Repeat / Remix / Kill

Home should feel like:

Bloomberg terminal for creative decisions
but simplified for founders

Final dashboard definition

8’s dashboard is a revenue-driven creative lab. Home combines attention, commercial intent, and revenue into one decision screen; Create generates controlled carousel experiments; Results ranks creatives by money-intent instead of views; Memory stores what the project has learned; Competitors feeds research; Owl explains decisions without becoming the product.
