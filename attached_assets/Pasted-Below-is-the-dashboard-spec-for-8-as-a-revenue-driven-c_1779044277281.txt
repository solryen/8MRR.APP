Below is the dashboard spec for 8 as a revenue-driven content platform, not a generic social scheduler.

What competitors imply

Fastlane’s strongest pattern is simple: website in → product/audience/tone learned → swipe through generated content → schedule it. Their public site says Blitz Mode lets users swipe through thousands of generated pieces and fill a calendar.  ￼ Product Hunt describes the same idea: AI influencer content remixed from trending content, swipe-like selection, then scheduling to TikTok, Instagram Reels, and YouTube Shorts.  ￼

LarryLoop’s strongest pattern is the loop: generate content, post it, track what drives revenue, iterate winners.  ￼ Motion’s strongest pattern is creative analytics: grouping creatives to reveal winning patterns and momentum instead of judging isolated assets.  ￼ Foreplay’s strongest pattern is inspiration/research: save ads, track competitors, identify winning patterns, and turn insights into creative direction.  ￼

8 should combine those, but with one clear difference:

8 is not “make more content.” 8 is “make content that moves people closer to revenue, then prove what worked.”

Your uploaded Multireach spec already points in the right direction: its value is voice-of-customer research, intent-based visual matching, hook variations, and creative testing for operators managing multiple client/project outputs.  ￼

Dashboard evolution

Day 1

Dashboard says:

We understand your product.
Here is the first strategy.
Here are first inspiration patterns.
Generate your first batch.

After first posts

Dashboard says:

Add results.
We need data before learning.

After 5–10 posts

Dashboard says:

Early winner detected.
Generate more like this.

After 20–30 posts

Dashboard says:

Supported patterns found.
Kill weak angles.
Turn best pattern into ad candidate.

After revenue connection

Dashboard says:

This post made money.
This pattern is not just viral — it converts.

Final definition

8’s dashboard starts as a website-to-carousel lab and evolves into a revenue command center. On day one it shows strategy, inspiration, tracking setup, and first batch generation. As the user posts, it separates attention from buyer intent. As revenue connects, it ranks content by money generated and tells the user exactly what to make next.
